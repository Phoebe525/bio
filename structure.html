🧠 The Structure of the Nervous System

The nervous system is an intricate network that helps the body communicate and respond to its environment.

🔹 Central Nervous System (CNS): Includes the brain and spinal cord, responsible for processing and sending messages.

🔹 Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Consists of sensory and motor neurons that connect the CNS to the rest of the body.

The brain itself is divided into different regions responsible for various functions, such as:

  • Cerebrum: Controls thinking, memory, and voluntary movements.
  • Cerebellum: Helps with balance and coordination.
  • Brainstem: Regulates vital functions like breathing and heart rate.

🧩 Neurons: The Building Blocks of the Nervous System

Neurons are special cells that transmit electrical and chemical signals throughout the nervous system.

  • Dendrites: Receive signals from other neurons.
  • Axon: Sends signals away from the neuron.
  • Synapse: The gap between neurons where messages are transmitted.

These amazing cells allow us to move, think, and feel the world around us! ⚡
